Discussion of the Osborne Reynolds Number Experiment

1. What is the theoretical critical Reynolds number of internal flow in pipes? What does this quantity mean?

Answer: The critical Reynolds number for internal pipes is about 2300. This is means, at lower Re of 2300 we are more likely to observe laminar flow and above Re 4000 we are likely to observe turbulent flow.

Another reason might be the amount of ink being too much.

4. What is the physical mechanism that causes the flow to change from laminar to turbulent flow? How is it that flow can be laminar at very high Reynolds numbers?

Answer: Viscous forces in a laminar flow tend to be large relative to the inertial force. An increase in the Reynolds number causes the flow to transition from laminar to turbulent, and it is characterized by its unsteady, random, and chaotic attributes with a variety of eddy structures.

Answer: incorrect amount of ink, time error, volume error, vibrations due to the pump. error can be reduced by taking longer time in the reading, carefully adding the ink, isolating the vibration of the pump and using a less noise room..

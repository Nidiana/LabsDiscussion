📝 Data Collection Checkpoints

📏 About Uncertainty

Any measurement you take is only meaningful if you also report its uncertainty. Uncertainty shows how much doubt there is about the value you measured. With precision instruments, we still have measurement uncertainty - even a digital manometer reading of 87.43 mmH₂O has inherent uncertainty based on instrument resolution and measurement conditions.

Some of the calculation you need to complete are the following:

  • The Relative uncertainty for the raw data Find this box in the right side of the Data Record sheets
  • The Maximum and minimum values of flow rate. Read details at the back of the printout titled "for Post-processing"
  • The Average flow rate
  • The Flow rate uncertainty ∆Q(±)
  • The Flow rate relative uncertainty (∆Q / Q_avg × 100%)
